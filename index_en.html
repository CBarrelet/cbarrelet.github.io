About Me

My name is Cyril Barrelet, and I am currently serving as an ATER at Polytech Montpellier and the University of Montpellier, while pursuing a PhD in Robotics at LIRMM (Laboratory of Computer Science, Robotics and Microelectronics of Montpellier) within the ICAR team.

My PhD, supervised by Mr. Chaumont, Mr. Gouttefarde, Mr. Subsol, and Mr. Creuze, is titled "Machine Learning approaches for litter detection and classification in underwater videos for automated robotic seabed cleaning". This work is part of the European project MAELSTROM, which aims to clean the Venice lagoon using a robotic platform.

My research primarily focuses on computer vision applied to underwater object detection and on evaluating architectures on small datasets. I also explore issues related to domain adaptation and the discovery of new object classes.

In 2020, I collaborated as a research engineer on the BUBOT project, which aimed to identify, count, and measure fish on coral reefs using stereoscopic cameras.

In 2022, I co-supervised several internships, including:

  • "Tracking 3D movements of coral reef fishes using deep learning" (internship by H. Larbi)
  • "Re-Identification of Dogs on Video Cameras Using Deep Learning" (internship by E. Dias Ribeiro Neto) under the SEA-dog-SEA project.
  • "Visualization of the position and movements of stray dogs in Southeast Asia automatically identified from camera-trap images or videos" (internship by H. Duong) also under the SEA-dog-SEA project.
