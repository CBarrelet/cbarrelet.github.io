About Me

My name is Cyril Barrelet, and I am currently a Postdoctoral Researcher at ISEN Ouest on the Nantes campus since January 2026. I am working on the European project CARMEN, and my research focuses on biometrics, behavioral analysis, and privacy protection.

I obtained my PhD in Robotics and Intelligent Systems from the University of Montpellier in 2025. My doctoral work was supervised by M. Chaumont, M. Gouttefarde, G. Subsol, and V. Creuze, and is entitled "Machine Learning approaches for litter detection and classification in underwater videos for automated robotic seabed cleaning". This research was conducted within the framework of the European project MAELSTROM, which aims to detect, collect, sort, and recycle underwater waste in order to clean the Venice Lagoon using a robotic platform.

My doctoral research mainly focused on computer vision applied to object detection in underwater environments and on the evaluation of architectures on small datasets. I also explored domain adaptation issues and the discovery of novel object classes.

In 2020, I collaborated as a research engineer on the BUBOT project, which aimed to identify, count, and measure fish on coral reefs using stereoscopic cameras.

In 2022, I co-supervised several internships, including:

  • "Tracking 3D movements of coral reef fishes using deep learning" (internship by H. Larbi)
  • "Re-Identification of Dogs on Video Cameras Using Deep Learning" (internship by E. Dias Ribeiro Neto) under the SEA-dog-SEA project.
  • "Visualization of the position and movements of stray dogs in Southeast Asia automatically identified from camera-trap images or videos" (internship by H. Duong) also under the SEA-dog-SEA project.
